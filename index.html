About Me

I’m a public health professional who believes that public health is rooted in people and the communities where they live and work. I focus on grounded, actionable research and evaluation that leads to meaningful change, especially in areas like LGBTQ+ health disparities, harm reduction, and cannabis policy.

I recently completed my MPH in Social and Behavioral Sciences at Yale, where my thesis analyzed cannabis use patterns across sexual identities using national data. I’ve worked across sectors to turn research into action: at Bradbury-Sullivan LGBT Community Center, I’ve secured over $1 million in funding to expand health and community care access, at the Yale LGBTQ Mental Health Initiative, I supported clinical trials and led national recruitment strategies for psychotherapy research and through my evaluation work at Yale, I’ve helped strengthen public health programs by improving how we train and support the next generation of practitioners.

I also serve as a Parabola Leader at Parabola Center, where I help translate complex cannabis policy into accessible narratives that center equity and public health.

At the core of my work is a simple goal: bridging research and real-world impact—turning ideas into funding, research into action, and data into something that can actually make people’s lives better.
